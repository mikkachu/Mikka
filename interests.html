Interest!

Mikka Stasiuk obtains many interests. Her main interest is quantum mechanics. Quantum mechanics concerns the abstract behavior of particles and waves. Particles and waves inhabit the quantum realm; a scale smaller than the atomic scale. The laws in the quantum realm are governed by the Standard Model of Particle Physics (top image on the right). The Standard Model describes the universeâ€™s elementary particles as well as the three strongest forces out of the four fundamental forces. These forces include (in decreasing order of strength):

  • Strong nuclear force
  • Weak nuclear force
  • Electromagnetic force

In regard to particles, the Standard Model classifies three different types and additional particles within these classifications. The three classifications of particles within the Standard Model are:

  • Gauge bosons
  • Quarks
  • Leptons

The Gauge bosons include the photon, Z and W bosons and gluons. These particles obtain critical functions that act as three of the four fundamental forces. The photon is the particle of electromagnetic force; Z and W bosons of the weak force; and gluons of the strong force. As well, certain particles feel each force. The bottom table displays the particles feeling each force. 'Feeling' simply implies that the particles interact via the force. For example, electrons and quarks exchange photons with each other in order to establish the magnetic force governing the atom.

The Higgs Boson, otherwise known as "The god Particle," governs all matter by providing it with mass. Its associated field, the Higgs Field, stretches throughout the entire universe, interacting with all particles obtaining mass. Gluons and photons have no mass, thus they do not interact with the Higgs Field.
